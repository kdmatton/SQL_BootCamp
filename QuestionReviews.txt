PART 1:
Level 1: Core Queries
1. Full Table View: Write a query to display all columns and all rows from the Employees table.
2. Name Tags: Write a query that returns only FirstName, LastName, and JobTitle from the table.
3. Nuke It: Write the SQL command to permanently remove the Employees table from the database.

Level 2: Filtering & Aggregates
1. Salary Check: Write a query to find all employees who earn more than 50000.
2. Department Filter: Write a query to show all employees who work in the "Engineering" department.
3. Employee Count: Write a query that returns the total number of employees.
4. Unique Departments: Write a query that lists all departments in the table with no duplicates.

Level 3: Ordering & Limits
1. Top Earners: Write a query to list all employees sorted by Salary from highest to lowest.
2. Alphabet Soup: Write a query to show employees sorted by LastName in alphabetical order.
3. Department Order: Write a query to sort employees first by Department (A–Z) and then by Salary (highest first).

Level 1: Logical Filters on Steroids (AND / OR / NOT)
1. Find all customers who are older than 18 AND younger than 30, but NOT from Canada.
2. Show customers who are from United States OR Mexico, but NOT both.
3. Select customers whose FirstName starts with J OR M, but whose LastName does NOT contain son.
4. Find customers who are under 18 OR over 65, but NOT living in United States.
5. Show customers who are from Canada AND (Age > 21 OR Age IS NULL).

Level 2: BETWEEN — Inclusive, Exclusive, and NOT
1. Select customers whose Age is BETWEEN 20 and 29, inclusive.
2. Find customers whose Age is NOT BETWEEN 30 and 50.
3. Select customers who are BETWEEN 18 and 65, AND NOT BETWEEN 30 and 40.

Level 3: IN — Cleaner OR Logic
1. Find customers whose Country is IN (United States, Canada, Mexico).
2. Show customers whose Country is NOT IN (Ukraine, Russia).
3. Select customers who live IN (United States, Canada) AND Age > 21.
4. Select customers whose Age is IN (18, 21, 25) instead of using BETWEEN.

Phase 1: Comparing Data in the Same Table 
1. The “Same Country” Question:
How would you find customers who live in the same country as 
other customers?

2. The “Matching Last Names” Question:
How would you find customers that share the same last name with 
another customer?

3. The “Older Than” Question:
How would you find customers who are older than other customers 
in the table?

4. The “Age Difference” Question:
How would you find customers who are more than 10 years older
 than another customer?

5. The “Avoiding Duplicates” Question:
When comparing customers to each other, how would you prevent
 duplicate or reversed results?

Phase 2: Combining Results From Different Tables 
1. The “All Countries” Question:
How would you get a list of all countries from both customers 
and owners without duplicates?

2. The “Country and Age” Question:
How would you combine country and age data from customers and 
owners into one result?

3. The “Filtered Combine” Question:
How would you show customers under 21 and owners over 21 in 
a single result?

4. The “Source Label” Question:
How would you combine customers and owners while adding a 
column that shows whether the row came from a customer or an owner?

Phase 3: Summarizing Data
1. The “Customers per Country” Question:
How would you count how many customers live in 
each country?

3. The “Grouped Averages” Question:
How would you calculate the average age of customers for 
each country?

4. The “Sorting Groups” Question:
How would you list countries by the number of customers, 
from highest to lowest?
