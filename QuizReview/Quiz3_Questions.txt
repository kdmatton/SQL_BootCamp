Quiz 3

Phase 1: Modifying Existing Data (UPDATE & DELETE)

1. The “Profile Correction” Update:
A customer with ID 6 contacted support to fix outdated information.
How would you update their profile so their age is 35 and their country is Italy?

2. The “Birthday Passed” Adjustment:
John Doe just celebrated a birthday.
How would you increase his age by exactly one year without changing any other customers?

3. The “Test Account” Cleanup:
A fake account was created during development with a customer ID of 7.
How would you permanently remove this customer from the database?

4. he “Safe Mass Update” Scenario:
A regional change affects only customers with IDs greater than 4.
How would you update their country to Spain without touching earlier records?

5. The “Policy Violation” Removal:
Company rules state that users under 13 years old cannot remain registered.
How would you remove all customers who break this rule?

Phase 2: Controlling Result Size (LIMIT & OFFSET)

1. The “Homepage Preview” Query: The frontend team needs 
a small snapshot of customer data. How would you return 
only the first three customers?

2. The “Paginated Results” Query:
You’re building pagination for a customer list.
How would you skip the first two customers and return the next four?

Phase 3: Data Insights & Aggregation

1. The “Youngest Customer” Lookup:
Management wants to see who the youngest customer is, not just their age.
How would you return the first name, last name, and age of the youngest customer?

2. The “Oldest Customer” Report:
How would you return the first name, last name, and age of the oldest customer?

3. The “Demographic Summary” Task:
How would you:
Calculate the average age of all customers
Count the total number of customers
Count how many unique countries customers come from
Find the combined total age of all customers

Phase 4: Pattern Matching & Text Search

1. The “Starts With” Search:
Customer support needs a list of customers whose first name begins with the letter A.

2. The “Ends With” Search:
How would you find customers whose first name ends with the letter N?

3. The “Contains” Search:
How would you locate customers whose first name contains the letters “ar” anywhere?

4. The “Exact Length” Filter:
How would you find customers whose first name is exactly two characters long?

Phase 5: Cross-Table Relationships (Subqueries)

1. The “Active Customers” Query:
Sales wants to email customers who have placed at least one order.
How would you return only customers who appear in the orders table?

2. The “Inactive Customers” Query:
Marketing wants to target customers who have never placed an order.
How would you find them?

3. The “Valid Orders Only” Check:
An auditor wants to confirm all orders belong to existing customers.
How would you return only valid orders?

4. The “Order Reassignment” Fix:
An order was assigned to the wrong customer.
How would you update OrderID 1 so it belongs to CustomerID 3?
